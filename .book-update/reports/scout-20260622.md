## WAVE 2: Pedagogical-Depth Sweep

Forced-ranking across all 366 sections. Scores 0-6 (one point per marker: WHY framing, analogy, formalization+code, cross-chapter relation, tradeoffs, synthesis exercises).

### Part-by-Part Bottom 20%

**Part 1** — Weakest cluster: Module 2 sections 2.5-2.9 (CAP theorem, consensus, state-machine replication, quorum protocols, logging). All score 4-5 while the rest of Part 1 averages 5.8. They define concepts correctly but lack worked implementations — no toy Raft walkthrough, no quorum-write simulation, no CAP tradeoff spelled out with latency numbers. **Weakest: section 2.9** (summary/wrap-up, thin on formalization and tradeoff analysis).

**Part 2** — **Weakest: section 6.7** (MinHash & LSH, score 4). Formalizes Jaccard and the S-curve correctly but never explains WHY locality-sensitive hashing avoids the quadratic; the intuition pump for the banding threshold is missing. Section 6.9 (Fault Tolerance, score 4) treats lineage vs replication as assertion rather than tradeoff.

**Part 3** — **Weakest: section 10.4** (Asynchronous SGD, score 5). Introduces staleness τ and shows empirically that τ=20 costs 7.37x more steps, but never derives the functional relationship. Section 10.7 (compression) and 10.9 (lower bounds) also score 5: proofs are deferred or skipped entirely.

**Part 4** — Multiple genuine score-3 sections:
- **section 16.8** (Activation Offloading, score 3): mechanism named but not quantified; no worked cost model comparing recomputation vs CPU offload vs disk I/O
- **section 17.6** (MoE Load Imbalance & Dropout, score 3): symptoms named, load-balancing loss formula absent, no comparison of aux-loss vs bias-update strategies
- **section 19.4** (Deduplication & Quality Filtering, score 3): approaches listed, no MinHash/LSH algorithm, no quality-filter cost model, near-zero formalization — **single weakest in Part 4**
- section 18.3 (Deterministic Replay, score 4): what makes computation non-deterministic is never explained
- section 20.4 (V-trace/Q-λ, score 4): algorithms named, not derived; importance-sampling divergence not shown
- section 21.6 (Distributed Trial Scheduling, score 4): bin-packing framing absent; no scheduler comparison with latency numbers

**Part 5** — Weakest cluster in Chapters 23 and 26:
- **section 23.5** (Multi-Tenant Packing, score 3): one-sentence stub — mechanism promised, never delivered; **single weakest in Part 5**
- section 25.6 (Hybrid Search, score 3): no retrieval fusion formula, no worked example showing when hybrid beats pure-dense
- section 26.7 (Drift Detection, score 3): no statistical test (KS, Wasserstein, PSI), no false-positive cost model; "when to retrain" claimed but not formalized

**Part 6** — **Weakest: section 28.7** (Repeated Games & Learning Dynamics, score 4). Announces the Folk Theorem and lists learning dynamics (no-regret learning, fictitious play, replicator dynamics) but develops none. No code runs; no discount threshold δ* is computed. Section 28.6 (Mechanism Design) skips the dominant-strategy proof for second-price auctions. Section 32.1 (LLM Agents as Distributed Components) supplies only a deterministic stub, no real cost model.

**Part 7** — Uniformly strong (Ch 33 averages 5.5). **Weakest: section 33.9** (Managed Platforms, score 4): reads as a platform feature-tour (Databricks/SageMaker/Vertex); break-even formula is elegant but sensitivity analysis is absent; exercises are "plug in numbers" not "trace why the tradeoff matters."

**Part 8** — Uniformly deep (all sections 5-6). No genuine score-3 sections. Weakest by relative comparison: section 40.8 (Context & Memory) leans on architectural pattern description over formalization.
